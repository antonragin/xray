### 5. SUMMARY OF CROSS-CUTTING THEMES (All 6 Angles)
Looking across the entire precursor design architecture (Macro, Asset Allocation, Risk, Income, Costs, and Performance), several universal themes must be embedded into the master system prompt:

**A. The April 2026 Temporal Anchor**
Every single module must act as if the clock is stopped in April 2026. Reports lacking this temporal awareness will fail the advisor credibility test instantly.

**B. "Structural Proxying" as the Default Engine**
Because the system often operates without granular, real-time time-series data for every asset, all modules (Risk, Performance, Yield) must default to *Structural Proxying*. The system must evaluate the *DNA* of the portfolio (e.g., "This fund behaves like an IMA-B index proxy") rather than relying on absent raw data. 

**C. The "CDI Gravity Well"**
Across Asset Allocation, Risk, and Performance, the CDI is the absolute center of the Brazilian financial universe. It is the ultimate opportunity cost. Every risk taken, fee paid, or return generated must be structurally justified as a premium over the 14.75% risk-free rate. 

**D. Hyper-Localization of Terminology**
The system must inherently speak "Faria Lima" (the Brazilian financial district). Guardrails must enforce the use of local conventions: using *Copom* instead of "the central bank," *Selic/CDI* instead of "base rates," *NTN-Bs/IMA-B* instead of "inflation-linked bonds," and *Fundo Multimercado (FIM)* instead of "hedge funds." 

**E. Anti-Boilerplate Guardrails**
The most severe failure state for the LLM is generating generic Western financial advice (e.g., standard 60/40 logic) or static narratives. Brazilian fixed income is highly active, and the master system must actively suppress generic statements in favor of dynamic, inflection-point analysis.
