### 1. What Fee Structures Were Missed? (Brazilian Specifics)
Beyond standard management fees and the previously mentioned *come-cotas*, the Brazilian market has several idiosyncratic fee and tax structures that drastically impact net returns:

*   **Taxa de Custódia B3 (Tesouro Direto):** Investors often think Tesouro Direto is fee-free. While broker fees are usually zero, B3 charges a mandatory 0.20% annual custody fee. *Nuance:* Tesouro Selic is exempt up to R$ 10,000.
*   **Taxa de Performance (Marca d'água & Benchmarks):** Performance fees in Brazil typically require a high watermark (*marca d'água*). Missed nuance: The AI must identify *misaligned benchmarks*. For example, a Multimercado fund charging 20% over 100% of the CDI, or a Long-Biased fund charging performance over the CDI instead of Ibovespa/IPCA+Yield.
*   **Taxa de Carregamento (Entry/Exit Loads in Previdência):** Very common in older PGBL/VGBL products at traditional retail banks (*bancões*). You must check for both *taxa de entrada* (taken from every deposit) and *taxa de saída antecipada* (early exit penalties).
*   **Rebates / Comissionamento (CVM 179):** The new CVM transparency rule requires distributors to disclose how much they earn (ROA - Return on Assets) for distributing a fund. While not a direct fee deducted from the client's balance, high rebates indicate severe conflicts of interest.
*   **IOF Regressivo:** While technically a tax, IOF acts as a massive punitive fee on liquidity for the first 29 days of any Fixed Income or Multimercado investment. The precursor must flag trades/withdrawals mapped under 30 days.
*   **Taxa de Corretagem / Emolumentos:** For equities (Ações/FIIs/BDRs), while retail brokerages often offer "zero corretagem," B3 still charges *emolumentos* (around 0.03% per trade).

### 2. Should instruction text include typical fee ranges by instrument type?
**Yes, absolutely.** Providing baseline ranges allows the AI to shift from purely *descriptive* analysis to *diagnostic* analysis (flagging outliers and abusive products).

You should include this specific reference table in the AI’s system prompt:

*   **Fundos de Renda Fixa (FIRF):**
    *   *Acceptable:* 0.20% to 0.50% a.a. | *Outlier:* > 0.80% a.a. (often found in retail bank DI funds).
*   **Fundos Multimercado (FIM):**
    *   *Acceptable:* 1.00% to 2.00% a.a. mgt + 20% perf. over CDI. | *Outlier:* Management > 2.00% or performance without high watermark.
*   **Fundos de Ações (FIA):**
    *   *Acceptable:* 1.50% to 2.00% a.a. mgt + 15-20% perf. over Ibovespa/IBrX.
*   **Previdência Privada (PGBL/VGBL):**
    *   *Acceptable:* 0.50% to 1.50% a.a. (depending on risk), 0% Carregamento. | *Outlier:* Any taxa de carregamento > 0%, or fixed-income PGBLs charging > 1.00% a.a.
*   **Fundos Imobiliários (FIIs):**
    *   *Acceptable:* 0.80% to 1.20% a.a. combined (mgt + admin).

*Instruction to AI:* "If an asset's fee exceeds the 'Acceptable' upper bound for its category, generate a 'High Fee Warning' and calculate the projected 10-year compounding drag compared to a median-fee alternative."

### 3. Handling "Fee-Free" Spread-based Products (LCI/LCA/CDB)
The AI cannot calculate a "fee" if it's explicitly zero, but it *can* calculate **Opportunity Cost / Yield Drag**, which acts as the invisible fee.

**How to structure this in the precursor:**
1.  **Standardize to CDI-Equivalent:** Require the AI to convert the fixed income yield into a percentage of the CDI (e.g., a CDB paying 10% when the DI rate is 10.5% equals ~95% CDI).
2.  **Define a Benchmark Threshold:** Establish a "Fair Wholesale Rate" baseline for the current macroeconomic environment (e.g., for a 2-year CDB, a fair rate might be 100% CDI for top-tier banks, and 110% for mid-tier banks).
3.  **Calculate the Spread Drag:**
    *   *Formula:* (Benchmark Rate - Product Rate) = Invisible Spread.
    *   *Example:* If a retail bank offers a CDB at 85% CDI, and the market benchmark for that duration/risk is 100% CDI, the AI logs a "15% CDI Yield Drag" (which equates to roughly an invisible 1.5% a.a. fee in a 10% Selic environment).
4.  **Generative Disclaimer:** Instruct the AI to insert a standard disclosure in the report: *"Products like CDBs, LCIs, and LCAs do not charge explicit administration fees. Instead, the distributor is remunerated via a 'spread'—the difference between the rate the bank pays to borrow and the rate offered to you. Assets yielding below [X]% of CDI indicate high distributor spreads."*

**C. AI Diagnostic Flags (The "X-Ray")**

- [ ] **Alignment Check:** Flag performance fees tied to inappropriate benchmarks (e.g., Equity funds using CDI).
