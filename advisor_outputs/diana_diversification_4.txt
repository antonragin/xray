Here is the finalized diversification precursor design, resolving the conflicts between structural rigor (Cloe) and localized Brazilian market pragmatism (Diana), optimized specifically for deterministic downstream LLM generation.

### 1. Conflict Resolution (Optimizing for Deterministic AI Output)

**Conflict A: HHI/Effective-N (Cloe) vs. Simple Top-X Framing (Diana)**
*   **Resolution:** **Diana’s approach (Top-1, Top-3, Top-5)** is the primary choice, augmented by Cloe’s machine-readable thresholds.
*   **Justification for AI Output:** LLMs are notoriously poor at reliable, complex zero-shot arithmetic like calculating HHI (sum of squared market shares). Requesting HHI calculations increases hallucination risk. Summing the Top-1, Top-3, and Top-5 positions is computationally simple, deterministic, and maps directly to how Brazilian advisors actually communicate. We will retain *Effective N* only as a background metric for internal systemic triggers, not for LLM natural language generation.

**Conflict B: Detailed Vehicle Taxonomy (Cloe) vs. Practical BDR/Feeder Cases (Diana)**
*   **Resolution:** **Diana’s practical approach** combined with Cloe’s `lookthrough_level` boolean flags.
*   **Justification for AI Output:** Overly granular taxonomies confuse LLMs (e.g., struggling to differentiate between a "structured note" and a "certificate of deposit" if metadata overlaps). A shallow taxonomy anchored to concrete Brazilian vehicles (e.g., *FIC FI*, *BDR*, *ETF*) paired with an explicit, deterministic `lookthrough_required: TRUE/FALSE` flag forces the AI to check underlying assets without guessing vehicle mechanics based on abstract definitions.

**Conflict C: Abstract Format (Cloe) vs. Brazil-Specific Anchoring (Diana)**
*   **Resolution:** **Cloe’s structure populated strictly with Diana’s content.** 
*   **Justification for AI Output:** LLMs drift when given abstract rules (e.g., "Warn if concentration is high"). They become deterministic when given structured, numerical anchors (e.g., "Warn if bank concentration > 20% per CVM 175"). Using Cloe’s `[Scope][Use for][Interpret carefully][Do not infer]` format prevents instruction-following drift, but injecting Diana's exact Brazilian parameters (FGC, CVM, Tesouro) prevents cultural hallucination (like an AI flagging a 50% *Tesouro Nacional* position as a dangerous concentration, which is false in Brazil).

### 2. Final Checklist (Priority Order for Implementation)

For an implementer to verify completeness of the diversification precursor, it must contain the following blocks:

1.  **Applicability & Coverage Matrix**
    *   [ ] Each dimension (Issuer, Exposure, Country) explicitly mapped as `covered`, `unknown`, or `not_applicable`.
2.  **Top-X Concentration Metrics**
    *   [ ] Computed `top_1`, `top_3`, and `top_5` concentration percentages per dimension.
3.  **Entity Resolution & FGC Mapping**
    *   [ ] Exact issuer mapped to Parent Conglomerate (CPF/CNPJ level).
    *   [ ] FGC eligibility flag (`eligible` / `ineligible`).
    *   [ ] FGC limit threshold calculation (Warn if > R$250k per conglomerate, hard cap at R$1M).
4.  **CVM 175 & Asset Allocation Heuristics**
    *   [ ] Concentration flag: Bank/Financial Institution > 20% (Trigger warning).
    *   [ ] Concentration flag: Corporate/Credit > 10% (Trigger warning).
    *   [ ] Sovereign Debt baseline flag: Tesouro Nacional mapped to "Normal Baseline" (Do not warn up to 60%).
5.  **Lookthrough & Country Mechanics**
    *   [ ] `lookthrough_level` flag indicating if the vehicle is a wrapper (e.g., BDR, FIC).
    *   [ ] Country risk split into: `vehicle_domicile` (e.g., BR), `listing_venue` (e.g., B3), `country_of_risk` (e.g., US).
6.  **Behavioral/Tax Bias Flag**
    *   [ ] Tax-exempt vehicle flag (LCI/LCA/CRI/CRA) explicitly tagged as "Monitor for tax-driven over-concentration."

### 3. Final Instruction Text Templates (The Patterns)

These templates merge Cloe’s structure with Diana’s Brazilian parameters. Operators will fill in the `{placeholder}` variables for specific records.

**1. instrument_template (Vehicle Mechanics)**
> **[Scope]** Brazilian Wrapper & Mechanics. Defines how the asset is structured legally. pt-BR label: `{pt_br_label}` (e.g., BDR, FIC FI).
> **[Use for]** Determining if a lookthrough is required to assess true diversification. `lookthrough_status`: `{lookthrough_boolean}`.
> **[Interpret carefully]** Ensure a distinction between local wrapper and underlying risk. A local ticker does not mean local economic risk.
> **[Do not infer]** Do not infer that `{pt_br_label}` guarantees liquidity or local sovereign backing. 

**2. economic_exposure (What Risk)**
> **[Scope]** Underlying Asset Class Risk. pt-BR label: `{pt_br_label}` (e.g., Renda Fixa Pós-Fixada, Renda Variável).
> **[Use for]** Evaluating macro portfolio allocation against Brazilian baselines.
> **[Interpret carefully]** If `{pt_br_label}` is Sovereign Debt (Tesouro Direto/Nacional), allocations up to `{safe_limit}%` (e.g., 60%) are standard in Brazil and MUST NOT be flagged as dangerous concentration.
> **[Do not infer]** Do not infer correlation between different economic exposures without checking the `issuer_type`.

**3. issuer_type (Issuer Family / Sector)**
> **[Scope]** Broad category of the entity issuing the risk. pt-BR label: `{pt_br_label}` (e.g., Instituição Financeira, Empresa Privada).
> **[Use for]** Applying CVM 175 concentration heuristics. Warn if concentration in `{issuer_type}` exceeds `{cvm_limit}%` (e.g., 20% for Banks, 10% for Corporates).
> **[Interpret carefully]** High concentration in a single issuer type implies sector risk, even if individual issuers are diversified.
> **[Do not infer]** Do not infer FGC guarantee based purely on issuer type; evaluate at the specific instrument and issuer level.

**4. issuer (Exact Entity / Conglomerate)**
> **[Scope]** The exact legal entity or financial conglomerate responsible for the payout. pt-BR label: `{pt_br_label}`.
> **[Use for]** FGC (Fundo Garantidor de Créditos) tracking. Aggregating all exposures to `{conglomerate_name}`.
> **[Interpret carefully]** Warn immediately if exposure to `{conglomerate_name}` exceeds R$250.000 per CPF, up to the R$1.000.000 global cap, for FGC-eligible assets.
> **[Do not infer]** Do not treat subsidiaries of `{conglomerate_name}` as separate entities for diversification purposes.

**5. tax_profile (Tax Bucket & Behavioral Bias)**
> **[Scope]** Tax treatment of the asset for Brazilian individuals. pt-BR label: `{pt_br_label}` (e.g., Isento de IR, Tributado).
> **[Use for]** Identifying tax-driven behavioral biases. If `{pt_br_label}` is "Isento" (LCI/LCA/CRI/CRA), analyze if the investor has sacrificed diversification to chase tax exemptions.
> **[Interpret carefully]** Tax exemption often masks high issuer concentration. Highlight this trade-off to the advisor.
> **[Do not infer]** Do not infer that tax-exempt assets are lower risk; they are purely a tax mechanism.

### 4. Recommended Ordering for Prompt Concatenation

To optimize against LLM "Lost in the Middle" syndrome and build logical context hierarchically, the instruction blocks should be concatenated into the precursor in this exact order:

**1. `instrument_template` (The Wrapper)**
*   *Why First?* It dictates how the LLM should read the rest of the data. If the asset is a BDR or Feeder Fund (FIC), the LLM immediately knows to look through the domestic shell to the data below. It sets the `country_of_risk` vs `vehicle_domicile` context immediately.

**2. `issuer` (The Exact Entity & FGC)**
*   *Why Second?* Issuer collapse is the most acute risk. Placing the exact conglomerate name and the hard FGC limits (R$250k/R$1M) early ensures the LLM's attention is captured by the most critical legal and capital-loss guardrails.

**3. `issuer_type` (The Sector / CVM Limits)**
*   *Why Third?* Broadens the lens from the specific entity to the sector. Applies the CVM 175 regulatory limits (10% / 20%).

**4. `economic_exposure` (The Macro Risk)**
*   *Why Fourth?* Establishes the overarching asset class (e.g., Sovereign vs Corporate). It is placed here so the LLM can apply the "Tesouro Nacional exception" (allowing 40-60% concentration) after understanding the specific vehicles.

**5. `tax_profile` (The Behavioral Bias)**
*   *Why Last?* This is the "narrative" layer. Once the AI understands *what* the instrument is, *who* issued it, and its *macro risk*, the tax profile gives the AI the "why" (e.g., "The client is highly concentrated in Bank X's credit risk [Issuer/Issuer Type] because they chased tax-free yield [Tax Profile]"). Placing it last perfectly sets up the LLM to generate the final advisory narrative.
