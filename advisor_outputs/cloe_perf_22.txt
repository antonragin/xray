global_guideline_for_instructions_performance: |
  When no product-level data, portfolio holdings, benchmark definition, dates, hedge policy, fees, or tax details are available, the AI may explain performance mechanics in generic terms, using conditional language such as "typically", "tends to", "may", and "if unhedged / if held to maturity".
  
  The AI must clearly distinguish conceptual explanation from realized attribution. It must not invent returns, benchmark capture, alpha, duration, contribution, or tax impact. It must not imply certainty, guarantee, or principal protection. It must not say what actually happened in a given period unless the relevant data is provided.

fi_cdi_br:
  instructions_performance: |
    CAN SAY:
    - Explain that, in Brazilian pós-fixado fixed income, performance is usually driven mainly by CDI accrual over time ("carry").
    - Mention that net performance may differ from CDI because of management/performance fees, credit spread income or losses, defaults, cash drag, fund expenses, and taxation.
    - Explain that some CDI-linked products target a percentage of CDI or CDI plus a spread, and that this changes expected gross carry.
    - Note that if the portfolio also holds private credit, longer-dated assets, or derivatives, mark-to-market and spread opening/tightening may affect returns.

    MUST NOT SAY:
    - Do not state the realized return, exact % of CDI, exact spread over CDI, or alpha versus benchmark without data.
    - Do not imply the product necessarily matches CDI, beats CDI, or is "risk-free".
    - Do not assume there is no volatility, no credit risk, or no mark-to-market just because the strategy is CDI-linked.
    - Do not quantify carry, fee drag, or credit contribution without fund and period information.
