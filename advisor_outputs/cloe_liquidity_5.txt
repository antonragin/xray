Yes. I’d model liquidity on **two separate axes**:

1. **Contractual time-to-cash** for instruments with maturity.  
2. **Structural exit mechanism** for instruments without maturity.

That matters here because, in your sample, only **45%** of portfolio weight has `expiry_date`; a maturity-only view misses the other **55%**.

## 1) Additional derived fields the precursor should compute mechanically

### A. Position-level derived fields
These are the most useful additions.

- `has_defined_maturity` : boolean  
  `true` when `expiry_date` exists.

- `liquidity_profile_code` : enum  
  This is the key new classifier. Suggested values:
  - `dated_cashflow`
  - `bank_paper_lockup_or_maturity`
  - `treasury_daily_buyback_mtm`
  - `exchange_secondary_market`
  - `fund_open_redemption`
  - `fund_closed_secondary_market`
  - `unknown`

- `exit_mechanism_code` : enum  
  Slightly more operational than profile:
  - `hold_to_maturity`
  - `daily_buyback`
  - `secondary_market_sale`
  - `fund_redemption`
  - `unknown`

- `liquidity_horizon_bucket` : enum  
  This replaces the temptation to misuse `maturity_bucket` for undated assets:
  - `same_day_or_daily`
  - `d_plus_1_to_5`
  - `d_plus_6_to_30`
  - `over_30_business_days`
  - `at_maturity`
  - `market_dependent`
  - `unknown`

- `market_sale_required` : boolean  
  True for equities, ETFs, FIIs, and other positions where exit requires a buyer.

- `mark_to_market_on_exit` : boolean  
  Critical for Tesouro Direto and market-traded instruments. Tesouro Direto offers daily repurchase, but early sale happens at market price, so realized proceeds before maturity can differ from the originally contracted return. ([tesourodireto.com.br](https://www.tesourodireto.com.br/es/sobre-o-tesouro/regras-e-regulamento))

- `lockup_present` : boolean  
- `lockup_days_min` : integer|null  
- `lockup_status` : enum (`none`, `present_known_days`, `present_unknown_days`, `unknown`)  
  Good for LCI/LCA and some funds.

- `fund_redemption_quote_days` : integer|null  
- `fund_redemption_payment_days` : integer|null  
- `fund_redemption_total_days` : integer|null  
  For open funds, the relevant dates are request date, quote/conversion date, and payment date; these terms must be in the fund’s regulation/lâmina, and payment is capped by regulation at up to 5 business days after conversion. ([gov.br](https://www.gov.br/investidor/pt-br/investir/tipos-de-investimentos/fundos-de-investimentos/como-investir-aplicacao-e-resgate))

- `fgc_eligible` : boolean  
  I would normalize current blanks to explicit `false` or `null`.

- `liquidity_assessment_basis` : constant enum  
  Example: `structural_only`  
  This is important so downstream AI does **not** imply observed market liquidity.

- `instruction_parse_status` : enum  
  (`parsed`, `partial`, `unparsed`)

- `instruction_tags` : array of strings  
  Example: `["daily_buyback","mtm","fgc"]`

### B. Portfolio-level derived fields
These should go straight into the bundle.

- `dated_weight_pct`  
- `undated_weight_pct`

- `weighted_avg_days_to_expiry_dated`
- `weighted_avg_years_to_expiry_dated`

- `maturity_bucket_weights`
- `largest_maturity_bucket_weight_total`
- `largest_maturity_bucket_share_of_dated`
- `maturity_bucket_hhi` or another concentration metric

For your sample:
- largest bucket on total portfolio = **18%**
- largest bucket as share of dated sleeve = **40%**

- `fgc_eligible_weight_pct`
- `exchange_traded_weight_pct`
- `daily_buyback_weight_pct`
- `fund_redemption_weight_pct`
- `market_dependent_exit_weight_pct`

- `fund_redemption_weighted_avg_total_days`

### C. One thing I would **not** do
Do **not** compute a single “liquidity score” unless it is clearly labeled as **structural** and decomposable.  
With no spreads, no trading volume, and no market depth, a single score will usually overstate precision.

Avoid `% in liquid instruments`.  
Use:
- `% in exchange_traded instruments`
- `% with daily buyback`
- `% subject to fund redemption`
- `% requiring market sale`

That is more honest given the absence of market data.

## 2) For positions without `expiry_date`, what replaces `maturity_bucket`?

Do **not** force a maturity surrogate.

Use:

- `maturity_bucket = null`
- plus **one new primary field**: `liquidity_profile_code`

And ideally also:

- `exit_mechanism_code`
- `liquidity_horizon_bucket`

### Recommended mapping
- **Stocks** → `liquidity_profile_code = "exchange_secondary_market"`  
  Public equities are exited by selling to another investor in the secondary market. ([gov.br](https://www.gov.br/investidor/pt-br/investir/tipos-de-investimentos/acoes/como-investir))

- **ETFs** → `exchange_secondary_market`  
  ETFs are exchange-traded and can be bought/sold in the secondary market like stocks. ([b3.com.br](https://www.b3.com.br/pt_br/produtos-e-servicos/negociacao/moedas/etf.htm))

- **FII** → `fund_closed_secondary_market`  
  FIIs are closed-end funds; cotistas generally do not redeem with the administrator and instead exit via secondary-market sale. ([gov.br](https://www.gov.br/investidor/pt-br/investir/tipos-de-investimentos/fundos-de-investimentos-imobiliarios-fii/como-negociar))

- **Open-end funds / previdência funds** → `fund_open_redemption`  
  Liquidity comes from fund redemption terms, not maturity. ([gov.br](https://www.gov.br/investidor/pt-br/investir/tipos-de-investimentos/fundos-de-investimentos/como-investir-aplicacao-e-resgate))

- **Tesouro Direto** → `treasury_daily_buyback_mtm`  
  Daily structural exit exists, but proceeds before maturity are market-priced. ([tesourodireto.com.br](https://www.tesourodireto.com.br/es/sobre-o-tesouro/regras-e-regulamento))

So the replacement is **not another maturity field**. It is an **exit-path field**.

## 3) Should the precursor compute a portfolio-level “FGC-covered percentage”?

**Yes — but name it carefully.**

I recommend:

- `fgc_eligible_weight_pct`

Not:

- `fgc_covered_pct`

Why: FGC protection is capped **per CPF/CNPJ and institution/conglomerate** (up to R$250k, with an overall R$1 million cap in four years), and only certain products are eligible. CDB, LCI and LCA are eligible; Tesouro Direto, fundos and debêntures are not in ordinary FGC coverage. ([fgc.org.br](https://fgc.org.br/sobre-garantia-fgc))

So with your current data you can compute:

```text
fgc_eligible_weight_pct = sum(weight where has_fgc == true)
```

For your sample, that is **18%**.

But you should **not** imply that 18% is fully guaranteed in economic terms, because that would require:
- BRL position sizes,
- issuer/conglomerate aggregation,
- possibly joint-account logic,
- and reliable issuer coverage.

So:
- **Yes**: include `fgc_eligible_weight_pct`
- **No**: do not call it “covered” unless you later compute caps by conglomerate and investor.

## 4) How to capture Brazilian structural liquidity characteristics using only instruction text

Best design: use `instructions_liquidity` as a **versioned rule-carrier**, then parse it deterministically.

### Best practice
Store instruction text in a **controlled mini-grammar**, not only prose.

Example:

```text
profile=treasury_daily_buyback_mtm; exit=daily_buyback; mtm=true; fgc=false
```

or, if you still want human-readable text:

```text
Liquidez diária via recompra do Tesouro Direto; venda antecipada sujeita à marcação a mercado. | tags: profile=treasury_daily_buyback_mtm; exit=daily_buyback; mtm=true
```

Then the precursor parses the tags mechanically.

### What to encode by instrument family

#### LCI / LCA
Use instruction text to encode:
- `profile=bank_paper_lockup_or_maturity`
- `fgc=true`
- `lockup_present=true`
- `lockup_days_min=<n or null>`
- `lockup_rule_source=legal_or_contractual`

Why text-based and versioned? Because the legal minimum terms were changed by CMN in 2024: the minimum maturity for new LCA emissions was extended from 90 days to 9 months, and for LCI from 90 days to 12 months. That is exactly the kind of rule you want in a reference table, not hard-coded inside model logic. ([gov.br](https://www.gov.br/fazenda/pt-br/assuntos/noticias/2024/fevereiro/cmn-ajusta-lastros-elegiveis-e-prazos-de-vencimento-dos-titulos-incentivados))

**Important limitation:** with only current fields, you usually **cannot compute remaining carência** unless you also know issue/acquisition date. So capture:
- presence of lockup,
- minimum term if parseable,
- but leave `lockup_days_remaining = null` unless another source exists.

#### Tesouro Direto
Encode:
- `profile=treasury_daily_buyback_mtm`
- `exit=daily_buyback`
- `mark_to_market_on_exit=true`

Tesouro Direto offers daily liquidity/repurchase, but early sale is at market value rather than guaranteed hold-to-maturity economics. ([tesourodireto.com.br](https://www.tesourodireto.com.br/es/sobre-o-tesouro/regras-e-regulamento))

#### Open-end funds
Encode:
- `profile=fund_open_redemption`
- `exit=fund_redemption`
- `fund_redemption_quote_days`
- `fund_redemption_payment_days`
- `fund_redemption_total_days`
- `lockup_present`
- optional `gates_possible=true`

Open funds disclose redemption timing in the regulation and lâmina; regulation also allows carência and, in exceptional illiquidity cases, temporary closure/barriers to redemptions. ([gov.br](https://www.gov.br/investidor/pt-br/investir/tipos-de-investimentos/fundos-de-investimentos/como-investir-aplicacao-e-resgate))

#### FII
Encode:
- `profile=fund_closed_secondary_market`
- `exit=secondary_market_sale`
- `admin_redemption_available=false`

FIIs are closed-end; exit is normally by selling to another investor in secondary markets. ([gov.br](https://www.gov.br/investidor/pt-br/investir/tipos-de-investimentos/fundos-de-investimentos-imobiliarios-fii/como-negociar))

#### Stocks / listed ETFs / crypto ETFs
Encode:
- `profile=exchange_secondary_market`
- `exit=secondary_market_sale`
- `market_sale_required=true`
- `market_depth_unobserved=true`

Stocks are exited in secondary markets, and ETFs trade in exchange as well. ([gov.br](https://www.gov.br/investidor/pt-br/investir/tipos-de-investimentos/acoes/como-investir))

### If you cannot change instruction text format
Then use deterministic regex extraction from prose, e.g. detect:

- `liquidez diária`
- `marcação a mercado`
- `carência`
- `D+(\d+)`
- `cotização`
- `pagamento do resgate`
- `mercado secundário`
- `FGC`

But tagged text is much safer than free prose.

## My concise recommendation

If you want the cleanest design:

1. **Keep `maturity_bucket` only for dated assets.**  
2. **Add `liquidity_profile_code` for all assets.**  
3. **Compute portfolio metrics separately for**
   - maturity,
   - FGC eligibility,
   - exchange-traded exit,
   - fund-redemption exit,
   - daily buyback exit,
   - lockup exposure.  
4. **Put structured tags inside `instructions_liquidity`** and parse them deterministically.  
5. **Expose coverage explicitly** so downstream AI knows which statements are maturity-based vs structurally inferred.
