### 1. Brazilian-Specific Risk Factors Missed
Beyond your initial list, a Brazilian portfolio has deep structural risks that require **zero market data** to identify. You must add:

*   **Indexation Mismatch (Risco de Indexador):** The split between **CDI** (floating), **IPCA/IGP-M** (inflation-linked), and **PRE** (fixed rate). A portfolio might be balanced in maturity but massively exposed if inflation spikes and it is 100% PRE.
*   **Tax/Regulatory Risk (Risco Tributário):** 
    *   *Come-Cotas:* Open-ended mutual funds suffer semi-annual tax bites (May/November) which destroy long-term compounding compared to tax-exempt assets.
    *   *IOF & IR Regressivo:* The sliding scale of Brazilian income tax (22.5% to 15% over 720 days) and IOF (which zeroes out only after 30 days). Liquidating early is a massive structural risk.
    *   *Isenção:* Exposure to tax-exempt assets for individuals (LCI, LCA, CRI, CRA, Debêntures Incentivadas).
*   **Liquidity Red Tape (Risco de Resgate D+N):** Brazilian funds often have aggressive redemption mismatch (e.g., D+30, D+60, or even D+180 for Multimercados/Ações). 
*   **FGC Conglomerate Limits:** The FGC (Credit Guarantee Fund) covers up to R$ 250k *per conglomerate*, capped at R$ 1MM globally across 4 years. If a portfolio has R$ 200k in Banco Itaú and R$ 100k in Itaú Corretora, it breaches the single conglomerate limit.
*   **Structured Product Subordination:** For FIDC (Receivables Funds), the distinction between *Cotas Sênior* (safest), *Cotas Mezanino*, and *Cotas Subordinadas* (first-loss piece).

### 2. Proxies to Prevent Hallucination (No Market Data)
Without market data (no covariance matrices, no live DI curve, no current yield-to-maturity), the AI will try to invent risk numbers to fill the void. You must substitute quantitative metrics with **deterministic structural proxies**:

| Traditional Metric (Requires Market Data) | Structural Proxy (No Market Data Needed) |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Volatility / VaR** | **Asset Class CVM Classification:** Group by CVM mandate (Renda Fixa Simples = Level 1, Multimercado = Level 3, Ações = Level 5). |
| **Duration / Convexity** | **Weighted Average Days to Maturity (WAM):** Calculated purely from issuance metadata. Group into buckets: `<1yr`, `1-3yr`, `3yr+`. |
| **Yield to Maturity / Expected Return** | **Contractual Indexation Matrix:** Output exact percentages: `60% CDI`, `30% IPCA+`, `10% PRE`. |
| **Credit Spread / Default Prob.** | **FGC Coverage % + Issuer Concentration:** e.g., "40% Uninsured Credit, 25% concentrated in Issuer [X]". |
| **Liquidity Risk / Bid-Ask Spread** | **Weighted Average D+N (Days to Cash):** The aggregate time it takes for cash to hit the account based on formal fund/asset rules. |
