Yes — but **split “explicit recurring fees” from “tax/timing/transaction costs.”** That separation is the key design choice.

## 1) What should the precursor compute?

**Yes, compute these core outputs:**

1. **Weighted average explicit annual fee (%)**
   - Formula: `sum(weight * yearly_fee_pct)`
   - This is your main portfolio fee number.

2. **Same number in bps**
   - Formula: `sum(weight * yearly_fee_pct * 100)`
   - If `yearly_fee_pct` is stored as percent (e.g. `0.2300` = `0.23%`), then bps = pct × 100.

3. **Fee contribution by holding / category**
   - Better than “fee-weighted allocation.”
   - Formula per holding: `weight * yearly_fee_pct`
   - This tells you which positions drive fee drag.

4. **Fee distribution data**
   - By **allocation bucket**: e.g. 0 bps, 1–25 bps, 26–50 bps, 51–100 bps, 101–150 bps, 150+ bps.
   - By **instrument_kind**
   - By **tax_profile_code**
   - By **known-fee vs blank-fee coverage**

5. **Performance-fee exposure, separately**
   - `performance_fee_exposed_weight`
   - `weighted_avg_performance_fee_pct_on_exposed_weight`
   - Do **not** add performance fees into the annual fee estimate; under CVM 175 they are conditional, benchmark-linked, charged after expenses, and generally no more frequently than semiannually. ([conteudo.cvm.gov.br](https://conteudo.cvm.gov.br/cvm_institucional/export/sites/cvm/legislacao/resolucoes/anexos/100/resol175.pdf))

6. **Coverage stats**
   - `weight_with_yearly_fee_populated`
   - `weight_with_blank_yearly_fee`
   - `weight_with_performance_fee`
   - This is mandatory, because blank does **not** always mean costless.

### On your sample CSV
Using your data as-is:

- **Weighted avg explicit annual fee** = **0.3420%**
- **Total estimated explicit annual fee** = **34.2 bps**
- **Weight with populated yearly_fee_pct** = **50%**
- **Weighted avg fee on fee-populated sleeve only** = **0.6840%**
- **Performance-fee-exposed allocation** = **7%** at **20% performance fee**

So I would show **both**:
- **Captured explicit recurring fee:** 34.2 bps
- **Fee coverage:** only 50% of allocation has a populated annual fee field

That prevents false precision.

## 2) How should instructions handle direct bonds / treasury / implicit costs?

The instruction text should say:

> **Blank yearly_fee_pct does not necessarily mean “free.” It means no deterministic recurring template fee was encoded. Other costs may still exist and are not included in the portfolio annual fee estimate.**

For Brazilian direct fixed income, distinguish 3 things:

### A. **Recurring explicit charges**
For **Tesouro Direto**, there is an explicit **B3 custody fee of 0.20% a.a.** on Tesouro Selic, Tesouro IPCA and Tesouro Prefixado; for **Tesouro Selic**, the first **R$10,000 per CPF is exempt**, and intermediaries may also charge their own administration fee, which is optional and varies by institution. ([tesourodireto.com.br](https://www.tesourodireto.com.br/es/sobre-o-tesouro/regras-e-regulamento))

### B. **Transaction / market costs**
Brokerage, B3 emoluments, spread, and mark-to-market on early sale are **not annual recurring product fees** and should not be forced into `yearly_fee_pct`. For Tesouro Direto, early sale also exposes the investor to market price variation. ([tesourodireto.com.br](https://www.tesourodireto.com.br/es/sobre-o-tesouro/regras-e-regulamento))

### C. **Embedded economics**
For CDB/LCI/debênture bought directly, the bank/dealer spread is usually embedded in the quoted rate/price; it is not an annual management fee.

### Recommendation
- Keep `yearly_fee_pct` focused on **explicit recurring product fee**
- Use `instructions_fees` for **“other costs not included”**
- For Tesouro templates specifically, add a note because the custody charge is real but not cleanly representable with one universal number

A good template note would be:

> “This template does not include all possible costs. In Tesouro Direto, B3 custody may apply (0.20% a.a.; Tesouro Selic has exemption up to R$10,000 per CPF on the exempt portion), and the intermediary may charge its own administration fee. Early sale may also involve spread/market-price effects and short-term taxes.”

## 3) Should the fees precursor include come-cotas interaction?

**No, not in the fee number.**  
Come-cotas is a **tax**, not a fee, and without returns, holding period, and fund tax classification you cannot convert it into a reliable annual fee-equivalent.

What the fees precursor **should** do:

- keep **fee metrics pure**
- add a **tax interaction flag/note**
- push actual modeling to the tax precursor

Why:
- Under current Receita guidance, periodic taxation happens on the **last business day of May and November** for funds under the general regime; long-term funds are generally taxed at **15%** in the periodic collection and short-term funds at **20%**, with complementary tax on exit where applicable. ([gov.br](https://www.gov.br/receitafederal/pt-br/centrais-de-conteudo/publicacoes/manuais/orientacao-tributaria/fundosdeinvestimento))
- But **not every fund** is subject to periodic taxation. Receita states that **FIA**, and when they meet the relevant conditions **FIP, FIDC, and ETFs except fixed-income ETFs**, are outside periodic taxation; FII/Fiagro follow separate rules. ([gov.br](https://www.gov.br/receitafederal/pt-br/centrais-de-conteudo/publicacoes/manuais/orientacao-tributaria/fundosdeinvestimento))

So **`tax_profile_code = general` is too coarse to infer come-cotas safely**.  
I would add an enum like:
- `come_cotas_status = applies | does_not_apply | unknown`

But keep it **outside** the fee total.

## 4) What should Brazilian-specific instruction text convey?

It should convey 4 things:

### A. Your fee model is simplified
ANBIMA’s transparency regime breaks fund remuneration into **gestão, administração, taxa máxima de distribuição**, and for previdência, **taxa de estruturação de previdência**; disclosure can be segregated or shown as a global fee with decomposition. ([anbima.com.br](https://www.anbima.com.br/data/files/F3/D2/96/96/70F6A9100414E6A9F82BA2A8/Regras_Procedimentos_Codigo_AGRT_v_10_11_2025_.pdf))

So your `yearly_fee_pct` should be described as:

> “simplified rolled-up recurring annual fee captured from the template, not a full regulatory decomposition.”

### B. CVM 175 documents can show more than your model captures
CVM 175 materials for FIFs include fields such as **taxa de performance**, **taxa máxima de custódia**, and a **taxa total de despesas** / expense simulation in disclosure materials. ([conteudo.cvm.gov.br](https://conteudo.cvm.gov.br/cvm_institucional/export/sites/cvm/legislacao/resolucoes/anexos/100/resol175.pdf))

So instruction text should warn:

> “Actual regulated fund documents may disclose additional fee components and total expense information beyond the two template fields used here.”

### C. Performance fee is conditional
CVM 175 sets specific constraints around performance fees, including reference-index rules, minimum charging periodicity, charging after expenses, and no charge below quota base. ([conteudo.cvm.gov.br](https://conteudo.cvm.gov.br/cvm_institucional/export/sites/cvm/legislacao/resolucoes/anexos/100/resol175.pdf))

So instruction text should say:

> “Performance fee percentages indicate possible contingent charges and are not included in the estimated annual recurring fee.”

### D. IOF is tax, not fee
For short-term exits, IOF applies to redemptions before 30 days and is charged on **gains**, not principal; in Tesouro Direto, the same short-term IOF logic applies. ([tesourodireto.com.br](https://www.tesourodireto.com.br/b/impostos-e-taxas-no-tesouro-direto))

So instruction text should say:

> “IOF on short-term exits is a tax on gains and is excluded from annual fee estimates.”
