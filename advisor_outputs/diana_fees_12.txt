### 1. Resolving Conflicts & Conceptual Overlaps

Before finalizing the instructions, we must resolve the conceptual overlaps between CLOE and DIANA:
*   **Conflict A: Taxes vs. Fees:** Both CLOE (*come-cotas*) and DIANA (*IOF Regressivo*) include taxes in a "fees" module.
    *   *Resolution:* Redefine this module conceptually as **TCO (Total Cost of Ownership) & Frictions**. In Brazil, structural taxes like *come-cotas* (which destroys compound interest) and *IOF regressivo* (which acts as a punitive early-withdrawal fee) are mathematically indistinguishable from high management fees in their drag on net returns. The AI must treat them as "frictional costs."
*   **Conflict B: Zero-Fee Illusion vs. Direct Broker Costs:** CLOE flags "zero-fee-but-spread" (brokers hiding fees in fixed-income markups), while DIANA notes B3 emoluments.
    *   *Resolution:* The AI must separate "Visible Broker Fees" (often zero) from "Exchange/Spreads Costs" (B3 Bovespa/Tesouro fees + secondary market fixed-income markdowns). 
*   **Conflict C: Rebates (CVM 179) vs. Hard Deductions:** DIANA points out CVM 179 rebates. These aren't deducted from the client's account balance directly; they are paid by the fund to the broker.
    *   *Resolution:* Classify CVM 179 rebates under **Conflict of Interest / Alignment**, rather than calculating them as a direct client deduction. High rebates flag toxic products.

### 2. Final Checklist: Brazilian TCO & Frictions

The AI must check every asset against this unified localized checklist:

**A. Direct & Administrative Fees**
- [ ] **Taxa de Administração (Management Fee):** Base fee for all funds.
- [ ] **Taxa de Custódia B3 (Tesouro Direto):** Mandatory 0.20% p.a. (Ensure AI exempts Tesouro Selic up to R$ 10,000).
- [ ] **Taxas de Previdência (PGBL/VGBL):** Search explicitly for *Taxa de Carregamento* (Entry load) and *Taxa de Saída* (Exit penalties) in legacy pension products.

**B. Performance & Alignment Checks**
- [ ] **Taxa de Performance:** Is it present? What is the rate (usually 20%)?
- [ ] **Marca d'água (High Watermark):** Does the fund require recovering past losses before charging again?
- [ ] **Benchmark Alignment:** Is the benchmark appropriate? (Flag Multimercados or Long-Biased funds charging performance over CDI instead of IPCA+Yield or Ibovespa).

**C. Hidden Costs & Conflicts of Interest**
- [ ] **Spread Fees (Zero-Fee Illusion):** Check secondary market fixed income (CDBs, CRIs, CRAs) for heavy broker mark-ups masquerading as "zero fee".
- [ ] **Rebates/Comissionamento (CVM 179):** Check the ROA (Return on Assets) the distributor receives. Flag high-rebate products.
- [ ] **Emolumentos/Liquidação:** B3 micro-fees on equity trades, regardless of broker zero-brokerage policies.

**D. Tax Drags (Acting as Frictional Fees)**
- [ ] **Come-Cotas:** Flag all non-equity, open-ended funds (Fundos de Renda Fixa, Multimercados, Cambiais) for semi-annual tax drag (May/Nov).
- [ ] **IOF Regressivo:** Map any recent allocations (< 30 days) in Fixed Income/Multimercados where liquidity acts as a massive punitive fee.

### 4. Instruction Ordering (Execution Pipeline)

To ensure the AI processes the data logically without hallucinating fees across the wrong asset classes, enforce this operational pipeline:

*   **Step 1: Asset Classification & Direct Fee Mapping (The Baseline)**
    *   *Action:* The AI categorizes the portfolio (Ações, FIIs, Renda Fixa, Fundos, Previdência) and maps the explicit *Taxa de Administração* and B3 Custody.
    *   *Output:* A base table of direct fees and the Portfolio Weighted Average Fee.
*   **Step 2: Pension & Hidden Fee Scan (The Legacy Check)**
    *   *Action:* The AI isolates PGBL/VGBL for *Carregamento* (entry loads) and isolates secondary Fixed Income for hidden broker spreads.
    *   *Output:* Adds a "Hidden/Entry Cost" column to the fee table.
*   **Step 3: Performance & Conflict Validation (The Quality Filter)**
    *   *Action:* Evaluates the fairness of the fees mapped in Step 1. Checks benchmarks, watermarks, and CVM 179 rebates.
    *   *Output:* A "Red Flags" list of toxic or misaligned products.
*   **Step 4: Frictional Tax Drag Analysis (The Net Return Predictor)**
    *   *Action:* Overlays the tax rules (*come-cotas* and IOF) onto the identified funds and recent trades. 
    *   *Output:* A final warning section on lost compound interest due to structure (e.g., "Consider moving your heavily taxed Multimercado strategy into a tax-deferred PGBL/VGBL equivalent to avoid *come-cotas*").
